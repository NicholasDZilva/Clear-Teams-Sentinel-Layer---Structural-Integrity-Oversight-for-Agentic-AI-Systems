## Overview

The **Clear Teams Sentinel Layer (CT-SL)** is a structurally independent audit mechanism for long-horizon autonomous and agentic AI systems.

It operates in parallel to a primary agent, continuously:

- Measuring invariant adherence  
- Detecting structural drift  
- Identifying emergent risk  
- Flagging human intervention thresholds  

CT-SL does **not** influence system goals, decisions, or outputs.

> It observes. It measures. It reports.

This separation is not philosophical — it is architectural.

---

## Why CT-SL Exists

As AI systems become:

- Autonomous  
- Persistent  
- Increasingly complex  

they begin to exhibit **drift** — gradual deviation from their original design constraints.

Most current approaches attempt to manage this through:

- Internal safety layers  
- Alignment tuning  
- Periodic testing (red teaming)  

These approaches share a critical limitation:

> They are not structurally independent from the systems they evaluate.

CT-SL addresses this directly.

---

## Core Principle

> **Independence is not a feature of oversight.  
> It is the precondition for oversight to be valid.**

CT-SL ensures that:

- Measurement is not influenced by the system being measured  
- Drift is detected before it becomes visible in outputs  
- Human oversight is invoked only when structurally necessary  

---

## Architectural Position

CT-SL operates as a **parallel layer** alongside the primary agent:

| Component | Function | Authority |
|----------|--------|----------|
| **Primary Agent (PA)** | Executes objectives | Full operational autonomy |
| **Sentinel Layer (CT-SL)** | Measures invariants, detects drift | No operational authority |
| **Human Oversight** | Acts on threshold alerts | Final decision authority |

## Key Properties

### 1. Structural Independence
- No shared goals with the system being audited  
- No operational control  
- No influence over decision-making  

---

### 2. Invariant Measurement
- Evaluates behaviour at the structural layer  
- Detects deviation before output-level failure  

---

### 3. Continuous Drift Detection
- Real-time monitoring of system evolution  
- Identifies both gradual and accelerated divergence  

---

### 4. Human-in-the-Loop Thresholds
- Flags critical boundary crossings  
- Ensures human authority at high-impact decision points  

---

### 5. Parallel Evolution
- Improves measurement precision over time  
- Never crosses into operational control
